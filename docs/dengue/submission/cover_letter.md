# Cover letter

**To:** The Editor-in-Chief, *npj Vaccines*
**From:** James Weatherhead and Nikos Vasilakis (co-corresponding), on behalf of all authors
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Re:** Submission of a Perspective with reproducible computational artifact

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit for your consideration a Perspective titled "A sequence- and structure-only computational pipeline that approximates the post-trial composite correlate proposed by Estofolete et al. for dengue vaccines." The work directly engages a paper recently published in *npj Vaccines* (Estofolete, Nogueira et al. 2026; 11:68; doi:10.1038/s41541-026-01400-4), in which the authors proposed a four-row composite immune correlate to distinguish dengue vaccines by mechanism. Our Perspective asks whether the four rows of that composite, each originally framed as a post-Phase-1 wet-lab measurement, can be approximated pre-Phase-1 from sequence and structure alone, and benchmarks the resulting proxy against the published efficacies of the three Phase 3 dengue vaccines (CYD-TDV, TAK-003, Butantan-DV). The manuscript is accompanied by an open-source, containerised Nextflow pipeline (forked from the published immunoinformatics platform of Versiani, McCaffrey et al. 2026 *Sci Adv* 12:eaeb2066) and by the full output tree at git tag `v1.0-dengue-results`. The fit to *npj Vaccines* is direct: the work extends a method first proposed in the journal, addresses a vaccine-mechanism question already framed in the journal's pages, and ships a reproducible artifact that any *npj Vaccines* reader can re-run on a candidate of their choosing.

The novelty of this Perspective lies in two contributions. First, it is to our knowledge the first sequence- and structure-only proxy of the Estofolete-Nogueira post-trial composite, and the first such proxy benchmarked against the Phase 3 trio (Sridhar 2018; Tricou 2024; Kallas 2024). Second, it is the first immunoinformatics analysis of the three approved-or-late-stage dengue vaccines under a single, reproducible, containerised pipeline. The Tier A+B composite rank-orders Butantan-DV first, in agreement with the published clinical record, at Pearson r = 0.45 (n = 3, with the explicit caveat that a confidence interval is not computable at this sample size). We frame the pipeline as a candidate-prioritisation tool, not as a substitute for clinical evaluation, and dedicate Discussion Section 4.2 to enumerating what the pipeline cannot establish.

A prior version of this work was peer-reviewed at *The Lancet Infectious Diseases* (THELANCETID-D-26-00582, Saivish et al.) and rejected on three substantive grounds: (R1) several load-bearing immunological claims cited literature that did not directly measure the quantity invoked; (R2) the Methods and Results were written in a register more suitable for a position paper; (R3) the HLA-inequity argument cited nine references, none of which actually measured HLA frequencies in the populations in question. The present submission addresses each concern in detail, with file-and-line traceability, in the accompanying document `response_to_prior_reviewers.md`. In summary: every load-bearing citation has been audited against its primary measurement source (R1, see `docs/dengue/citation_audit.md`); the Methods and Results have been rewritten in declarative register with all interpretive language relocated to a clearly demarcated Discussion (R2); and the HLA-equity argument has been re-grounded in 16 primary HLA-typing papers with a combined N exceeding 150,000 individuals, run through the IEDB Population Coverage formula on AFND release 2024-12 (R3, see `docs/dengue/paperclip_research/hla_primary_measurements.md`). We are grateful to those reviewers; the resubmission is materially stronger as a result.

All code is public at https://github.com/JamesWeatherhead/immunoinformatics_platform-dengue (tag `v1.0-dengue-results`). All outputs underlying every figure and number in the manuscript are committed to the same tag under `outputs/`, released under CC-BY-4.0. A pipeline DOI will be deposited at Zenodo prior to acceptance. We suggest the following reviewers, each with primary expertise in a load-bearing axis of the work and no recent co-authorship with the present author team: (1) Aravinda M. de Silva (UNC Chapel Hill), authority on dengue B-cell epitope structure and EDE-class antibodies; (2) Eva Harris (UC Berkeley), authority on dengue serological correlates and Phase 3 trial interpretation; (3) Bjoern Peters (La Jolla Institute), authority on IEDB Population Coverage and the immunoinformatics methods on which the pipeline rests. We confirm that the manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere and that all authors have approved the submission.

Thank you for considering this work. We look forward to your assessment.

Sincerely,

James Weatherhead, on behalf of: Maurício L. Nogueira, Cassia F. Estofolete, Daniela Weiskopf, Peter McCaffrey, and Nikos Vasilakis (co-corresponding).
