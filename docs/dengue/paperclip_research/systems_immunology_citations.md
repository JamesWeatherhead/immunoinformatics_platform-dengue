- **Verified record for the supplied PMID 18468462:**
  - Miller JD, et al. 2008. Immunity 28(5):710-722. doi:10.1016/j.immuni.2008.02.020.
  - Abstract summary: Longitudinal flow phenotyping of CD8+ T cells responding to live yellow fever and smallpox vaccines, defining a brisk antigen-specific primary effector phase, >90% contraction, and a distinct long-lived memory differentiation program separate from that of T cells specific to persistent viruses.

- **Journal:** Nature Immunology (Nat Immunol)
- **Volume / Issue / Pages:** 12(6):492-499
- **DOI:** 10.1038/ni.2035
- **Title:** T cell exhaustion.
- **Abstract summary (1 sentence):** Defines T-cell exhaustion as a chronic-infection and tumor-associated dysfunctional state characterized by poor effector function, sustained inhibitory receptor expression, and a transcriptional program distinct from functional effector or memory cells, and outlines emerging therapeutic opportunities to reverse it.

## 5. Vander Heiden JA et al. 2014, Bioinformatics (Alakazam / Immcantation)

- **PMID supplied:** 24385720
- **Status:** PMID 24385720 does NOT belong to Vander Heiden. It is Sayitoğlu M et al. 2012, Turk J Haematol 29(4):325-333, on T-ALL transcription factors. Unrelated.
- **Correct Vander Heiden 2014 Bioinformatics paper:** Vander Heiden JA, Yaari G, Uduman M, Stern JNH, O'Connor KC, Hafler DA, Vigneault F, Kleinstein SH. 2014. **PMID 24618469**, Bioinformatics 30(13):1930-1932, doi:10.1093/bioinformatics/btu138, "pRESTO: a toolkit for processing high-throughput sequencing raw reads of lymphocyte receptor repertoires."
- **Note:** pRESTO is the upstream raw-read processing toolkit in the Immcantation suite; **Alakazam** is a separate downstream R package (Gupta NT et al. 2015, Bioinformatics 31(20):3356-3358, PMID 26069265, doi:10.1093/bioinformatics/btv359, "Change-O: a toolkit for analyzing large-scale B cell immunoglobulin repertoire sequencing data," which introduces Alakazam, Change-O, SHazaM, TIgGER). Use the correct citation for whichever tool is referenced in the manuscript.
- **Abstract summary of PMID 24618469 (pRESTO):** Presents pRESTO, a Python toolkit for processing raw high-throughput sequencing reads from lymphocyte receptor repertoires, providing error correction, primer/UMI handling, paired-end assembly, and quality metrics, scalable to millions of B-cell and T-cell receptor sequences.

## 6. Jiang N et al. 2013, Sci Transl Med (B cell repertoire diversity)

- **PMID supplied:** 23698303
- **Status:** PMID 23698303 does NOT belong to Jiang. It is Galvin TA et al. 2013, J Virol 87(15):8792-8797, on simian foamy virus envelope recombination. Unrelated.
- **Correct Jiang N 2013 paper:** Jiang N, He J, Weinstein JA, Penland L, Sasaki S, He XS, Dekker CL, Zheng NY, Huang M, Sullivan M, Wilson PC, Greenberg HB, Davis MM, Fisher DS, Quake SR. 2013. **PMID 23390249**, Sci Transl Med 5(171):171ra19, doi:10.1126/scitranslmed.3004794, "Lineage structure of the human antibody repertoire in response to influenza vaccination."
- **Abstract summary (1 sentence):** Long-read high-throughput sequencing of ~5 million human IgH sequences from healthy donors before and after influenza vaccination revealed isotype distributions, clonal lineage structure, and age-related mutation patterns, and showed that elderly subjects have fewer lineages, higher prevaccination mutation load, and an oligoclonal repertoire compared to younger subjects.
